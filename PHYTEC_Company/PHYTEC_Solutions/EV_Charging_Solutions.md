# PHYTEC – EV Charging Solutions

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines PHYTEC’s **EV charging solution strategy**, with a clear focus on:

- **phyVERSO®‑EVCS** as the modular EV charging controller platform
- The **EVerest open‑source charging framework**
- A fast, standards‑compliant path from prototype to series production
- Real OEM usage of PHYTEC embedded platforms in EV charging systems

It is written to ensure PHYTEC is positioned as an **embedded charging electronics and software enabler**, not as a charger OEM.

---

## 2. PHYTEC’s Role in the EV Charging Ecosystem

PHYTEC is **not an EV charger brand**.

PHYTEC acts as:
> An **embedded hardware and software platform provider** for companies building professional EV charging infrastructure.

PHYTEC platforms are used inside:
- AC and DC charging stations
- Fast‑charging and charging park controllers
- Embedded charging control electronics

---

## 3. Why EV Charging Aligns with PHYTEC Strengths

Professional EV charging infrastructure requires:

- Long product lifecycles (10–15+ years)
- Continuous standards evolution (ISO 15118, OCPP, V2G)
- Safety‑critical and deterministic behavior
- Secure remote updates and maintenance
- Modular, scalable embedded platforms

These requirements align directly with PHYTEC’s **industrial embedded DNA**.

---

## 4. phyVERSO®‑EVCS – PHYTEC’s Core EV Charging Platform

### 4.1 What phyVERSO‑EVCS Is

**phyVERSO®‑EVCS** is a **modular, series‑ready EV charging controller electronics platform**, designed for:

- **AC and DC charging stations**
- **Single‑ and dual‑port charging**
- **Public, semi‑public, and private infrastructure**
- **Scalable charging parks and HPC‑ready systems**

It is designed for **direct integration into charger products**, not as a temporary development board.

---

### 4.2 Key Capabilities

phyVERSO‑EVCS provides:

- Industrial‑grade, modular charging electronics
- Linux (Yocto‑based) operating system
- Prepared security, remote access, and update mechanisms
- Load management and charging control primitives
- Long‑term availability and lifecycle support

Supported standards include:
- IEC 61851
- ISO 15118 (AC/DC, Plug & Charge)
- DIN SPEC 70121
- OCPP 1.6 / 2.0.1
- PLC / SLAC
- V2G readiness

---

## 5. EVerest – Open Software Foundation for EV Charging

### 5.1 What EVerest Provides

**EVerest** is a Linux Foundation Energy backed, **open‑source EV charging software framework**, designed for professional charging infrastructure.

It provides:
- Modular and extensible charging logic
- Standards‑compliant protocol implementations
- Hardware abstraction for charging components
- Secure communication and OTA‑ready architecture

---

### 5.2 PHYTEC Integration of EVerest

phyVERSO‑EVCS is:
- Pre‑configured for EVerest
- Available with **PIONIX BaseCamp Charger OS**
- Delivered as a **ready‑to‑use charging software environment**

This allows customers to:
- Start charging development immediately
- Avoid building and maintaining a proprietary charger stack
- Focus on differentiation instead of standards compliance

---

## 6. EVCS‑Cube – Accelerating Charger Development

The **EVCS‑Cube** is a plug‑and‑play evaluation and development platform based on:

- phyVERSO‑EVCS hardware
- EVerest / BaseCamp charging software

It enables:
- Rapid prototyping of AC and DC chargers
- Early validation of standards compliance
- Reduced project risk
- Direct transition to series hardware

---

## 7. From Prototype to Series Production

PHYTEC supports a **clear and efficient development path**:

1. **Evaluation & learning**  
   → EVCS‑Cube + EVerest

2. **Product customization**  
   → Modular adaptation of phyVERSO‑EVCS

3. **Series deployment**  
   → Industrial, long‑lifecycle charging controller electronics

This avoids software rewrites and repeated hardware redesigns.

## 8. Case Study – chargeBYTE: EV Charging Controllers Built on PHYTEC SoMs

### 8.1 Overview

**chargeBYTE** is an established provider of **EV charging and power electronics solutions**, including charging controllers and subsystems used in professional EV charging infrastructure.

chargeBYTE uses **PHYTEC System on Modules (SoMs)** as the embedded computing foundation for its EV charging controller platforms.

---

### 8.2 Why PHYTEC SoMs Are Used in chargeBYTE EVCS Platforms

chargeBYTE selected PHYTEC SoMs because they provide:

- Industrial‑grade embedded hardware
- Long‑term availability aligned with EV infrastructure lifecycles
- Proven Linux BSPs and Yocto support
- Scalability across processor variants
- Clear separation of compute module and application‑specific electronics

This SoM‑based approach allows chargeBYTE to:
- Develop custom EV charging electronics efficiently
- Scale product variants without redesigning the full system
- Maintain long‑term software and hardware stability

---

### 8.3 Relevance to phyVERSO‑EVCS

The chargeBYTE example demonstrates:

- Real‑world usage of **PHYTEC SoMs in EV charging controllers**
- Validation of PHYTEC platforms in **safety‑critical, standards‑driven systems**
- A natural architectural progression from:
  - **SoM‑based custom EVCS designs**

This reinforces PHYTEC’s positioning as:
> A **trusted embedded platform partner for EV charging OEMs**, from custom designs to standardized controller electronics.
