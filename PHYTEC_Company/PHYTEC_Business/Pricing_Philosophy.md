# PHYTEC – Pricing Philosophy

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines PHYTEC’s **pricing philosophy** and how pricing should be **positioned, explained, and justified** in customer discussions.

It ensures that:
- PHYTEC pricing is discussed consistently across teams
- Price is always framed in the context of **value, risk reduction, and lifecycle**
- Claude does not default to cost‑only or commodity‑style comparisons

This document does **not** contain price lists, discounts, or commercial terms.

---

## 2. Core Principle: PHYTEC Does Not Compete on Lowest Price

PHYTEC does **not** aim to be the lowest‑cost supplier in the embedded market.

Instead, PHYTEC pricing reflects:
- Industrial‑grade engineering
- Long‑term lifecycle commitments
- Manufacturing quality and control
- Software enablement and support
- Reduced total cost of ownership (TCO)

PHYTEC positions itself as a **value‑driven, not price‑driven** partner.

---

## 3. What PHYTEC Pricing Represents

PHYTEC pricing encapsulates **much more than hardware BOM cost**.

It includes:
- Decades of embedded design experience
- In‑house manufacturing and quality control
- Long‑term component and product lifecycle management
- Pre‑validated hardware platforms
- Industrial software enablement
- Global engineering support capability

Customers are not paying only for a board or module; they are paying for **risk reduction and predictability**.

---

## 4. Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Perspective

PHYTEC pricing should always be discussed in terms of **Total Cost of Ownership**, not unit price.

### 4.1 Typical Hidden Costs PHYTEC Helps Avoid

- Redesigns due to poor signal integrity or EMI issues
- Software instability over long lifecycles
- Component obsolescence surprises
- Quality issues from outsourced manufacturing
- Lack of long‑term software maintenance
- Internal engineering effort to “fix” platforms

Lower‑priced alternatives often shift these costs **back to the customer**.

---

## 5. Pricing and Lifecycle Commitment

A significant part of PHYTEC’s value lies in **long‑term availability and support**.

Pricing reflects:
- Commitment to multi‑year availability (often 10+ years)
- Proactive product lifecycle management
- Controlled change notifications
- Stable hardware and software baselines

PHYTEC avoids frequent, disruptive product changes that might lower short‑term cost but increase long‑term risk.

---

## 6. Pricing Across Product Types

### 6.1 System on Modules (phyCORE / phyFLEX)

SoM pricing reflects:
- High‑quality industrial components
- Scalability across product families
- Long‑term support obligations
- Suitability for medium to high production volumes

At scale, SoMs typically deliver **lower per‑unit cost** than SBCs while enabling maximum customization.

---

### 6.2 Single Board Computers (phyBOARD)

SBC pricing reflects:
- Fully integrated, production‑ready platforms
- Reduced NRE and faster time‑to‑market
- Suitability for low‑to‑medium volumes
- Use as reference platforms and COTS products

SBCs trade **higher per‑unit cost** for **speed and simplicity**.

---

### 6.3 Edge AI and Accelerated Platforms

Edge AI pricing reflects:
- Specialized AI hardware
- Dedicated memory and power design
- Software enablement complexity
- Long‑term industrial deployment suitability

PHYTEC does not position Edge AI platforms as low‑cost alternatives to cloud AI or consumer GPUs.

---

## 7. Software as Part of the Value, Not a Free Add‑On

PHYTEC pricing inherently includes **software competency**, even when software is not itemized separately.

This includes:
- BSP development and maintenance
- Yocto Project expertise
- Middleware integration
- Customer‑specific adaptations
- Long‑term software stability

Competitors who price hardware aggressively often recover cost through:
- Paid software support
- Consulting fees
- Limited maintenance windows

PHYTEC integrates software into its **value proposition**, not as an afterthought.

---

## 8. How to Respond to Price‑Focused Objections

When customers say:
> “Your solution is more expensive than X”

The correct response is **not** to defend the number.

Instead:
- Clarify lifecycle expectations
- Ask about volume, longevity, and support needs
- Explain what is included beyond the PCB
- Frame pricing in terms of avoided risk and engineering effort

Price objections often indicate **misaligned expectations**, not rejection.

---

## 9. What PHYTEC Avoids in Pricing Discussions

PHYTEC deliberately avoids:
- Race‑to‑the‑bottom pricing
- Feature‑only price comparisons
- Short‑term discounts that undermine long‑term trust
- Unclear or shifting pricing models

Consistency and transparency matter more than short‑term wins.

---

## 10. How Claude Should Talk About Pricing

When Claude is asked about pricing, it should:
1. Avoid specific numbers unless explicitly provided
2. Emphasize value, lifecycle, and risk reduction
3. Explain pricing differences structurally, not emotionally
4. Reframe the discussion toward total cost of ownership

Claude should **never**:
- Guess prices
- Compare PHYTEC purely on cost
- Suggest PHYTEC is a low‑cost supplier

---

## 11. Canonical Pricing Statement

> PHYTEC pricing reflects the cost of delivering **reliable, industrial‑grade embedded platforms with long‑term support**. While PHYTEC may not be the lowest‑priced option, it reduces overall project risk, engineering effort, and lifecycle cost — which is what matters in professional embedded products.

---

## 12. Summary

PHYTEC’s pricing philosophy is based on **value, predictability, and long‑term partnership**.

Customers who choose PHYTEC are not buying the cheapest hardware; they are investing in **stability, support, and execution certainty** over the full life of their product.
